Deposit Beta Calculation Methodology

Formula: Deposit Beta = (Œî Cost of IB Deposits) / (Œî Fed Funds Rate) Calculation: Œî Cost = 3.35% (Q2'25) - 0.33% (Q1'22) = 3.02% Œî Fed Funds = 5.50% (Q2'25) - 0.50% (Q1'22) = 5.00% IB-Only Beta = 3.02% / 5.00% = 0.604 (60.4%)

Key Observations: Beta Implications

Observation Implication
IB-Only Beta: 60.4% Higher than typical commercial banks (40-50%). CATY's institutional deposit base is highly rate-sensitive due to sophisticated treasury management by commercial clients.
All-In Beta: 50.7% DDA balances provide 960 bps of beta relief (60.4% - 50.7% = 9.7%). However, DDA mix declined from 24% (Q1'22) to 17% (Q2'25), reducing this cushion.
Up-Cycle Asymmetry Up-cycle beta of 50-60% suggests potential down-cycle beta of similar magnitude. Liability relief in a cutting cycle will be gradual.
NIM Sensitivity If Fed cuts 100 bps, CATY's deposit costs decline by ~50-60 bps (assuming symmetric beta), but asset yields also reprice. Net NIM impact depends on asset-liability duration gap.

Down-Cycle Beta Estimate & Forward Outlook

Analyst Estimate: Down-Cycle Beta Assumption

Based on CATY's institutional deposit franchise and peer benchmarking, we estimate a down-cycle deposit beta of 60%, assuming symmetric pass-through behavior. This reflects: (1) commercial depositors' active treasury management, (2) limited deposit stickiness in rate-cutting cycles, and (3) competitive pressure in California commercial banking market.

FTE (Fully Taxable Equivalent) Adjustments

FTE Methodology

CATY adjusts net interest income and NIM for the tax benefit of tax-exempt securities income. FTE adjustments increase reported NII to reflect the equivalent pre-tax yield required on taxable securities to match after-tax economics. This adjustment ensures comparability across institutions with varying tax-exempt security holdings.

FTE Adjustment Policy

Item Details
FTE Disclosure CATY discloses NIM on both a reported (GAAP) and FTE basis in MD&A average balance tables.
Tax Rate Assumption FTE adjustments typically assume a statutory federal tax rate (21%) plus applicable state taxes. CATY's effective tax rate disclosed in income statement notes.
Tax-Exempt Securities Primarily municipal bonds. CATY's tax-exempt securities portfolio size and composition disclosed in securities footnote (Note 3 - Investment Securities).
Materiality FTE adjustments typically add 5-15 bps to reported NIM, depending on tax-exempt securities mix. Institutional investors analyze NIM on FTE basis for peer comparisons.
