Capital Return Framework

Banking Sector Context
Banks like Cathay General Bancorp return capital to shareholders through two primary mechanisms:

1. Cash Dividends: Regular quarterly distributions representing a percentage of earnings
2. Share Repurchases: Buyback programs authorized by the Board to reduce share count and enhance per-share metrics

Key metrics for evaluating capital return include:
‚Ä¢ Dividend Payout Ratio: Dividends √∑ Net Income (typically 30-40% for banks)
‚Ä¢ Retention Rate: 1 - Payout Ratio (funds retained for organic growth)
‚Ä¢ Organic Growth Capacity: ROTE √ó Retention Rate (sustainable equity growth)
‚Ä¢ Total Shareholder Return: Dividend Yield + Share Price Appreciation

Capital Return Analysis Framework
Dividend Policy: Banks typically maintain stable or growing quarterly dividends, targeting 30-40% payout ratios to balance shareholder returns with capital retention for regulatory requirements and organic growth.

Buyback Policy: Share repurchases provide flexibility to return excess capital when regulatory capital ratios exceed targets and loan growth is moderate. Buybacks enhance EPS and ROTE by reducing equity base.

Organic Growth Capacity: The product of ROTE and retention rate determines sustainable equity growth without external capital raises:
‚Ä¢ If ROTE = 12% and Retention = 68%, organic growth capacity = 8.1%
‚Ä¢ This means equity can grow 8.1% annually from retained earnings alone
‚Ä¢ Actual growth may differ based on loan demand, M&A activity, and capital management decisions

Regulatory Constraints: Bank capital returns are subject to Federal Reserve stress testing (for larger banks) and must maintain Common Equity Tier 1 ratios above regulatory minimums (typically 7%+ including buffer).
