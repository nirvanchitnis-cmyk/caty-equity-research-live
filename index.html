Company Overview

Business Description

Cathay General Bancorp (NASDAQ: CATY) is the holding company for Cathay Bank, one of the largest Chinese-American banks in the United States. Founded in 1962 and headquartered in Los Angeles, California, Cathay Bank serves businesses and individuals with a strategic focus on the U.S.-China cross-border banking corridor and Asian-American communities.

The company operates approximately 60+ branches across California, New York, Texas, Washington, Illinois, Nevada, New Jersey, Hong Kong, Shanghai, and Taipei. Cathay Bank specializes in commercial real estate lending, commercial & industrial loans, residential mortgages, and trade finance for businesses engaged in U.S.-China commerce.

Revenue Model: Cathay generates revenue primarily through net interest income (93% of total revenue) from its loan portfolio, supplemented by noninterest income (7%) from service charges, wealth management fees, and trade finance services. The bank's NIM of 3.27% (Q2 2025) reflects strong asset pricing power and deposit franchise quality.

Customer Segments:

  • Commercial Banking (60-65% of loans): Middle-market companies, particularly Asian-American owned businesses, in industries such as real estate development, import/export, manufacturing, and professional services
  • Commercial Real Estate (52.4% of loans, Q2'25): Property-type composition NOT DISCLOSED; includes office, retail, multifamily, and industrial properties primarily in California, New York, and Texas metropolitan areas
  • Retail Banking (20-25% of loans): Residential mortgages, home equity lines, and consumer loans to affluent Asian-American households
  • Trade Finance: Letters of credit, foreign exchange, and cross-border payment services for businesses engaged in U.S.-China trade

Geographic Footprint: California remains the core market (50-60% of deposits and loans), with significant presence in New York (15-20%), Texas (10-15%), and other states. International offices in Greater China serve as referral hubs for U.S. business expansion.

  • Regulatory Scrutiny Post-Regional Bank Crisis: After Silicon Valley Bank/First Republic failures (2023), regulators increasing oversight of liquidity, unrealized securities losses, and commercial real estate concentrations
  • Deposit Franchise Quality Differentiation: Banks with sticky, low-cost deposits (operating accounts, treasury management relationships) outperforming vs those dependent on rate-sensitive CDs/money market accounts
  • AI/Technology Disruption: Generative AI enabling productivity gains in underwriting, customer service, and fraud detection. Banks investing in technology gaining efficiency advantages
  • M&A Activity Resuming: Regional bank consolidation accelerating as sub-scale banks (<$10B assets) struggle with regulatory costs and technology investments. Potential takeout candidates for larger regionals/national banks
