Thesis & Controversy

Investment Call: HOLD. The probability-weighted base path (50% probability: Fed eases 25–50 bps, credit contained) clusters at $43–45 intrinsic value versus the $46.17 spot. Guardrail, stress, and severe tails (30% / 15% / 5%) keep the skew negative, but the −4.6% expected gap is not wide enough to press a downgrade without Q3 10-Q proof of credit migration.

  • Base case (50%). FedWatch-weighted blend (−50/−25/0 bps) trims interest-bearing deposit costs ~22 bps and delivers quarterly EPS ~$1.16 before credit; after credit overlays we land at $1.03 (annual $4.12) and ROE ≈10.3% (probabilistic outlook).
  • Guardrail (30%). Through-cycle NCOs at 42.8 bps generate $12.4M incremental quarterly provisions (EPS $0.99), implying fair value mid-$43s; this is the scenario that keeps us from upgrading to BUY.
  • Stress (15%). Special-mention migration coupled with 85 bps NCOs pushes EPS to $0.75 and TBVPS $40.74; valuation support falls to high-$30s.
  • Severe (5%). GFC analogue at 120 bps NCOs drops quarterly EPS to $0.56 and TBVPS to $40.61, highlighting the need for watchlist transparency.
  • Capital/rate leverage. CET1 13.15% post-buyback leaves ~115 bps to the 12% internal floor; each additional $50M buyback consumes ~20 bps CET1 and halts if CET1 drifts below 12.3% (Module 10).

Valuation bands: Spot multiples (10.1× forward P/E, 1.27× P/TBV) exceed our probability-weighted and Monte Carlo bands. Guardrail, stress and severe scenarios imply fair values ≈$43/$39/$33, framing a committee range of $38–$45 that is too thin for a downgrade yet too weak for BUY.

  • Peer context. CATY trades at 1.28× P/TBV vs peer median 1.12× and 10.3× P/E vs peer median 11.1×; ROE ≈10.3% vs COE 9.99% leaves only +235 bps spread, below peers such as EWBC (ROE 16.1%). Holding pattern remains appropriate until we confirm either credit improvement or deterioration (Peer module).

Controversy & Disconfirmers: Credit migration and funding mix volatility could invalidate the base case.

  • Credit stress. NCOs jumping to 85 bps (movie theatre + CRE tail) drive quarterly EPS to $0.75 and TBVPS to $40.74; the severe case (120 bps) erodes EPS to $0.56 (Module 07).
  • Funding risk. DDA mix down to 17% with time deposits 47%; a +50 bps hike adds ~$13M interest expense per quarter and compresses ROE toward the 9.5% COE (Module 05).
  • Watchlist. Special-mention loans of $455M across six relationships would raise NPL ratio ~35 bps if 20% migrate; loan-level evidence and data gaps are detailed in Module 08 watchlist table.
