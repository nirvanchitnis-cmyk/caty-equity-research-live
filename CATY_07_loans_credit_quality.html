17-Year Average NCO Rate (2008-2024)

KEY FINDING: CATY's current NCO rate of 6.5 bps (Q2'25 annualized) is significantly below its through-cycle average of 42.8 bps. Normalizing credit costs to the through-cycle average is central to our HOLD rating, as it reduces normalized ROTE and implies significant downside to current valuation.

GFC Peak Context: During the 2009 Global Financial Crisis peak, CATY experienced 305.9 bps in net charge-offs - more than 7x the through-cycle average. This historical stress-test informs our recession scenario analysis.

Valuation Impact: Each 10 bps change in NCO assumptions impacts ROTE by approximately 20 bps and P/TBV by approximately 0.025x. Normalizing from 6.5 bps to 42.8 bps implies material compression in both profitability metrics and justified valuation multiples.

Valuation Implications of NCO Normalization

The through-cycle NCO rate of 42.8 bps is a critical input to our normalized ROTE and valuation framework. Current spot NCO rates significantly understate normalized credit costs, creating a false sense of profitability sustainability.

Provision Normalization

We use the through-cycle average of 42.8 bps as our baseline provision assumption for normalized ROTE calculation. This is conservative given CATY's commercial real estate concentration and recent portfolio growth.

42.8 bps baseline

ROTE Sensitivity

Each 10 bps increase in NCO assumptions reduces ROTE by approximately 20 bps. Moving from 6.5 bps to 42.8 bps (36.3 bps increase) implies ~73 bps reduction in normalized ROTE.

~20 bps / 10 bps NCO

P/TBV Impact

Each 10 bps change in NCO assumption impacts justified P/TBV by approximately 0.025x. The 36.3 bps normalization implies ~0.09x reduction in fair value P/TBV multiple.

~0.025x / 10 bps NCO

Recession Scenario

Using the GFC peak of 305.9 bps as a severe stress scenario, normalized ROTE would fall to approximately 4.2%, implying a P/TBV of ~0.25x at a 9.6% cost of equity.

4.2% ROTE | 0.25x P/TBV
