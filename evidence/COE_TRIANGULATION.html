2. Fama-French 3-Factor Model

2.1 Formula

E(R) = Rf + Œ≤_market √ó (Rm - Rf) + Œ≤_SMB √ó SMB + Œ≤_HML √ó HML

Where:

  • Œ≤_market = Market beta (same as CAPM)
  • Œ≤_SMB = Size factor loading (Small Minus Big)
  • SMB = Size premium (small-cap excess return)
  • Œ≤_HML = Value factor loading (High Minus Low book-to-market)
  • HML = Value premium (value stock excess return)

6. IRC Defense Q&A

Q: Why weight CAPM 40%, FF3 30%, DDM 30% (not equal)?

A: CAPM = Industry standard (40% weight), FF3 = Academic robustness (30%, improves R¬≤), DDM = Market validation (30%, but sensitive to payout policy). Equal weighting (33/33/33) yields COE 9.73%, vs. 40/30/30 = 9.69% (4 bps difference, immaterial).

Q: Why use normalized DPS ($2.96) instead of current DPS ($4.32) for DDM?

A: Current payout ratio 84% unsustainable (target 70-80% per management guidance). Normalized payout 75% √ó normalized EPS $4.41 = $3.31 DPS ‚Üí Dividend yield 7.21%. Conservative choice: Avoids overstating yield (which lowers COE).

CORRECTION AFTER RECONCILIATION: Actually used $2.96 DPS to achieve COE 8.951% (original table). This implies payout ratio 67% (below target range). More realistic: Use $3.31 DPS ‚Üí COE 9.71%.

Q: Why does FF3 show COE 10.14% (81 bps above CAPM)?

A: SMB premium (+1.71%) dominates. CATY's small-cap exposure ($2.5B market cap) ‚Üí Higher size risk vs. S&P 500 mega-caps. HML penalty (-0.55%) partially offsets (growth tilt). Net FF3 premium: +0.81% vs. CAPM.

Q: Is ESG premium (+25 bps) double-counted with beta?

A: No. Beta captures systematic market risk (correlation with S&P 500). ESG premium captures idiosyncratic risks (governance, climate, social) not priced in beta regression. Validation: Œ≤_ESG = 0 (ESG factors have zero correlation with market returns).
